Introduction

The aim of the study is to clarify if individuals identify objects or entities related to a category at a faster rate if they are typical examples of this category, as opposed to atypical examples. The study is being conducted by Andy Gibb at Glasgow Caledonian University. The study is being carried out by Andy Gibb as a part of an educational course for the award of BSc (hons) Applied Psychology.

 

Before you decide whether or not to take part, it is important for you to understand what participation in the study will involve. Please take time to read the following information carefully and discuss it with others if you wish. Please contact us at the address below if you would like more information.

 

Why is this study important?

This is an important study because it will help undergraduate students obtain a much better understanding of how research within the field of applied psychology is conducted. Conducting this study aims to foster skills to apply research methods to verify psychological theory, this in turn will help understanding of reading and appraising other research conducted in the field of psychology.

 

What will I have to do if I take part?

If you are interested in taking part, you will be invited to give consent. Giving consent will involve signing your initials in the Think Fast web application, just before you take the quiz/take part in the study. You will receive a copy of the signed consent form following your participation.

Once consent has been completed the application will begin a countdown before displaying a series of statements, one after another. You will be required to simply read the statement and determine if the statement is true or false. Statements do not necessarily have a right or wrong answer. But they are straightforward statements which are easy to understand. An example statement would be something like "The Sky is blue." This task can be completed on your own smartphone or touchscreen device. We also encourage you to not overthink any of the answers and invite you to compete with other participants to try and achieve the quickest time. Upon completing all of your assigned questions you will get to see the length of time it took you to answer the questions. Your results will automatically be collated and sent to the GCU Email address of Andy Gibb, who will inform you of your place on the leaderboard.

During the presentaton of findings, the winner with the shortest time will be awarded with a modest prize.

 

Do I have to take part?

No. You decide whether you want to take part. You can stop taking part in the study at any time, without giving a reason. Withdrawing from the study will not affect your medical care or legal rights. Any submissions to the Web application will be permanently deleted.

What are the possible risks with taking part?

All studies involve some level of risk and inconvenience. The possible risks involved with this study are your initials being leaked to a 3rd party, should the GCU email server security ever be compromised. Physical risks entirely fall to the device in which the web application is viewed upon. As this is a web application available via an HTTPS URL. You are required to provide your own device security.

 

What are the possible benefits of taking part?

We can’t promise the study will help you personally. However, the results should help our understanding of the theories of Plato's forms. This, in turn, is expected to be beneficial to the class in understanding Plato's role in his contribution to science.

 

What happens when the study stops?

Written reports of the study findings will be available from the 20th October 2025. However, a copy of the report can be requested from Andy Gibb.

 

What if there is a problem?

If you are concerned about your participation in the study and would like to speak with someone out with the study team, please contact Lesley Anne Macrae via email: L.MacRae@gcu.ac.uk.

 

What will happen to the information given during the study?

This section will explain what happens to the information you given during the study.

No personal data will be collected during this study. Response data from the SVT will be collated to look for common themes between response times. Anonymisation will occur purely on the basis that the only information that we collect from you will be your response times and your initials. No other information will be taken. The aforementioned information will only be accessed by the research group (Andy Gibb, Kai Gray, Heather Greene, Jenna Elliot) in the form of automated email. This information will be secured on the Glasgow Caledonian University email server which is password protected with Multi-factor Authentication. Data analysis will be conducted by Andy Gibb. Data will be destroyed (deleted from the email server) following presentation of findings after 20th October 2025

This study complies with the Data Protection Act (2018) and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). 

The data controller is Glasgow Caledonian University. Information is being processed on the basis of Article 6(1)(e) of the General Data Protection Regulation and to perform a task carried out in the public interest.

 

Enquiries specifically relating to data protection should be made to the University’s Data Protection Officer (DPO). The DPO can be contacted by email: dataprotection@gcu.ac.uk. If you are unhappy with the response from the University, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO). The ICO can be contacted by email: casework@ico.org.uk.

 

GDPR also gives study participants the right to ask for their personal data to be erased. If you would like us to stop using your personal data, then you can contact Andy Gibb (agibb14@caledonian.ac.uk) and ask for your personal data to be erased. However, it will only be possible to erase data that has not been anonymised and/or published. Further information about your rights can be found at: https://www.gcu.ac.uk/dataprotection/rights/
